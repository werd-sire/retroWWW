🔬 Research Philosophy

The Black Vault operates on the principle that government transparency is essential to democracy. Our research methodology is designed to distinguish between verified facts and speculation, providing the public with documented evidence rather than theories.

Core Principles:
  1. Primary Source Documentation - All claims must be supported by official documents
  2. Multiple Source Verification - Critical information requires corroboration
  3. Clear Attribution - Sources and methods must be explicitly documented
  4. Factual Accuracy - Speculation is clearly separated from verified information
  5. Source Protection - Whistleblower safety is paramount

📄 Document Verification Process

STEP PROCESS VERIFICATION METHOD CRITERIA
1 Source Authentication Verify document origin through agency markings, letterhead, classification stamps Official format, proper classification markings
2 Content Analysis Cross-reference with known historical events, personnel, and timeline consistency Historical accuracy, internal consistency
3 External Corroboration Compare with other declassified documents, Congressional reports, academic research Multiple independent confirmations
4 Expert Review Consultation with former government officials, historians, subject matter experts Professional assessment of authenticity
5 Publication Decision Risk assessment for national security vs. public interest Legal review, ethical considerations

VERIFICATION RATINGS:
★★★★★ FULLY VERIFIED - Multiple official sources, expert confirmation
★★★★☆ HIGHLY CREDIBLE - Official source with external corroboration
★★★☆☆ CREDIBLE - Single official source or multiple secondhand sources
★★☆☆☆ QUESTIONABLE - Limited verification, requires further investigation
★☆☆☆☆ UNVERIFIED - Insufficient evidence for publication

📝 FOIA Request Strategy

The Freedom of Information Act (1966) is our primary tool for obtaining government documents. Our systematic approach has achieved a 73% success rate over four years.

Request Preparation

1. Target Identification
• Research which agency likely holds records
• Identify specific time periods and programs
• Review previous releases for guidance

2. Request Crafting
• Use specific terminology from known documents
• Reference previously released material
• Request document categories, not just titles
• Include alternative search terms

3. Legal Framework
• Cite specific FOIA provisions
• Request fee waivers when appropriate
• Specify format preferences (electronic vs. paper)

Response Management

1. Tracking System
• Maintain database of all requests
• Monitor response deadlines
• Document agency correspondence

2. Appeal Process
• Challenge excessive redactions
• Appeal denials with specific legal arguments
• Escalate to Department of Justice when necessary

3. Litigation Support
• Partner with legal organizations
• Document agency non-compliance
• Support court cases with research

🛡️ Whistleblower & Source Protection

Protecting sources is both an ethical obligation and a practical necessity. Our protection protocols are designed to safeguard individuals who risk their careers and safety to expose government wrongdoing.

Communication Security

Initial Contact:
• Anonymous drop boxes
• Encrypted email systems
• Pay phone communications only
• Public meeting locations

Document Handling:
• No fingerprints or DNA traces
• Photocopies only (no originals)
• Chain of custody documentation
• Secure storage facilities

Identity Protection

Publication Standards:
• No identifying information
• Generic job descriptions
• Altered timelines when necessary
• Composite source creation

Legal Protection:
• Journalist shield law consultation
• Legal counsel for sources
• Whistleblower statute guidance
• Congressional contact facilitation

SECURITY WARNING: All electronic communications should be assumed to be monitored by government agencies. Sources should use only the most secure communication methods and avoid creating patterns that could identify them.

📊 Publication Standards

Every piece of information published by The Black Vault meets strict editorial standards designed to maintain credibility and accuracy.

Information Classification System

CATEGORY DESCRIPTION VERIFICATION REQUIRED PUBLICATION CRITERIA
CONFIRMED FACT Official government admission or multiple verified sources Primary documentation Immediate publication
HIGHLY CREDIBLE Single official source or multiple credible secondhand sources Expert review Publication with caveats
CREDIBLE ALLEGATION Whistleblower testimony with partial corroboration Background verification Clearly marked as allegation
UNVERIFIED CLAIM Single source without corroboration Source credibility assessment Research notes only
SPECULATION Analysis based on available evidence Logical reasoning assessment Clearly marked as analysis

Editorial Review Process

1. Initial Research Review - Accuracy of facts and sources
2. Legal Review - Classification issues and publication risks
3. Ethical Review - Public interest vs. privacy/security concerns
4. Expert Consultation - Subject matter expert verification
5. Final Editorial Decision - Publication, revision, or rejection
