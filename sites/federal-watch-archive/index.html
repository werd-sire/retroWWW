About Federal Watch Archive

The Federal Watch Archive represents fifteen years of rigorous, independent research into government accountability mechanisms and transparency policies. As a former senior analyst with the Defense Intelligence Agency, I bring professional analytical standards to the critical examination of how federal institutions serve the public interest.

This archive focuses on verified, documented evidence rather than speculation. All research is conducted using established intelligence community methodologies, with emphasis on source credibility, cross-verification, and factual accuracy.

Research Methodology

All Federal Watch Archive research adheres to intelligence community analytical standards:

  • Source Verification: Multiple independent confirmation required
  • Document Authentication: Official channels and provenance tracking
  • Analytical Objectivity: Clear separation of fact and assessment
  • Professional Standards: Peer review and academic quality control
