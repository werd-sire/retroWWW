Federal Watch Document Archive

The Federal Watch Archive maintains a verified collection of 3,247 government documents obtained through official channels including FOIA requests, congressional releases, and agency declassifications. All documents undergo authentication and source verification procedures.

Important Notice: Document Classification

All documents in this archive are unclassified or have been officially declassified through proper channels. The Federal Watch Archive does not seek, accept, or distribute classified information.

Document requests are processed in compliance with applicable laws regarding national security information, privacy rights, and source protection protocols.

Historical Document Collections

Cold War Oversight Evolution

Collection Size: 234 documents • Date Range: 1947-1991 • Status: Verified

Featured Documents:

  • National Security Act of 1947 and subsequent amendments
  • Pike Committee Report materials (1975-1976)
  • Intelligence Oversight Act of 1980 implementation records
  • Iran-Contra investigation congressional records
  • Post-Cold War intelligence community reform proposals

This collection documents the evolution of intelligence oversight from the National Security Act through the end of the Cold War, providing historical context for contemporary accountability mechanisms.

Document Access & Research Services

Qualified Researchers:
Academic researchers, journalists, and policy analysts may request access to full document collections upon verification of credentials and legitimate research purpose.

Access Procedures:

  1. Submit research proposal and credential verification
  2. Sign confidentiality agreement regarding source protection
  3. Specify document format requirements (digital/paper)
  4. Processing time: 3-5 business days for most requests

Fees:
No charges for research access. Reproduction fees may apply for large document orders (paper copies: $0.15/page; digital copies: no charge).

Research Assistance:
Dr. Caldwell provides consultation on document interpretation, source context, and analytical framework for qualified research projects.
